ABOUT US

Dare to Dream was born looking at the fact that the family is the basis of society, seeing the need that families need to spend more time together, the idea of creating activities for the whole family, enjoying and sharing moments that establish the bonds of family friendship and strengthen family ties, thus forming values that will be fundamental for a strong and healthy society. This will reduce juvenile delinquency, deaths, mental problems etc.

OBJECTIVES
  • The objectives of this work are oriented towards the improvement of teacher training, the study of Intercultural Education and its development through dance, music and theater.
  • According to these ideas, the following objectives are proposed:
    Make the performing arts a tool for Intercultural Education.
  • Interpret and create music using voice, classroom resources and instruments that can provide us, guitar, drums, keyboard.
  • Know your own voice and use the word and body as the noblest means of expression.
  • Find in your body (hands, voice, gesture, look, movements) communicative resources and enjoy them.
  • Learn to perform the costumes, scenery, props, objects, puppets, etc.
  • Enhance reading and correct diction defects.
  • Assimilating the problems of others having to take on their characters, like how to talk and feel according to its age and condition.
  • Analyze the characters and situations represented.
  • Make a critique of the dramatized fact.
  • Know how to collaborate with the costumes, decorations, manipulation of devices (tape recorder, lights ...), besides knowing how to behave properly in a show.
  • Know the resources of video recording as a summary and immortalization of a task.
  • Know the musical, corporal terms that are required and use them appropriately in the musical expression activities carried out in the classroom.
  • Know the artistic competences involved in the development of the intercultural model through theater, dance and music.
  • Establish the necessary guidelines or steps for the development of a creative process in which students and families collaborate together.
  • Develop the creativity of the student, encouraging the singing, theater or dance individually and in teams.
  • Consolidating the personality of the student responsibility and decision-making.
  • Apply performance processes in song, theater and dance, also apply specific techniques to play musical instruments, theater and dance.

Guitar Lessons

Objectives

  • Implement workshops on two levels: Basic and advanced.
  • The Basic level: it is oriented to those who do not know how to play guitar, and will start from the most fundamental part of the acoustic guitar.
  • The advanced level: is oriented to learn to play electric guitar and is aimed at those who have passed the basic level or already have notions of acoustic guitar.
  • The project aims to medium-term to encourage young people their interest in music, discover young people with natural talent for guitar and enhance their skills and create musical bands, have a rehearsal room equipped in the establishment, and so in this way to reach young people with limited resources or social risk to music and have an artistic and cultural space in the commune.

Benefits

  • In the first place, the workshop has the benefit of helping socially and will help young people stay away from alcohol, drugs and companies that lead them astray.
  • It is proven that learning to play a musical instrument significantly encourages increased self-esteem, which is also beneficial to resist group pressure and not fall into the vices mentioned above.
  • It will allow to expand their vision of themselves, their artistic abilities and a window will open to another world in which they can feel fulfilled.

Piano Lessons

Theory

Basic concepts essential to read and write music.

Lecture

Development of the ability to read at first sight through progressive exercises appropriate to the level of the student who present the difficulty necessary to simultaneously maintain interest and be overcome without difficulty.

Technical elements to be able to approach a score that presents greater reading complexity than that acquired through the reading exercises at first sight and to be able to decode, understand, memorize and execute the work through the analysis.

Writing

Apply reading knowledge to be able to write any musical idea.

Hearing Development

Through a gradual process, acquire the ability to auditively recognize all musical elements: instruments, melodies, scales, intervals, harmony, rhythms, etc ... oriented to develop the musical sense and at the same time to understand from all possible aspects The music that is heard.

Drum & Percussion

The central purpose of the classes is to provide the rhythmic-sound tools to establish a musical and interpersonal communication.

Classes are offered individually and or in groups, allowing to work in this way the interest or need of each particular student, considering their experience with the instrument.

The Latin American percussion study points to the particular knowledge of traditional instruments of each region and then be able to assemble them in the different folkloric rhythms.

Instruments

Technique

There are several techniques within the same instrument to execute it. Each of them allows us to develop speed, weight, intensity, volume, etc.

Personally, I consider that the more we include natural movements to our technical study, the more possibilities we have to bond and develop in a more conscious and relaxed way.

Remember that everything we play on an instrument in a context of improvisation, is all the technical development that the body already assimilates as its own.

Reading

Music is a language like any other with the characteristic of being universal, this means that the same score must be performed in the same way anywhere in the world, not including an aesthetic or cultural character that each region could accentuate.

As in the Spanish language there are letters that together put together words, which in turn form sentences, and combined with signs form paragraphs and texts, in music there are notes, figures and symbols that allow us to understand and execute works by other artists, as well as enhance the interpretation of our own instrument.

Improvisation

Improvisation is the area where, taking into account technical studies that allow us to develop minimally in the instrument, we are in contact with our emotional interior and learn to externalize it freely.

The important thing for me is to give the necessary tools to stimulate the discovery of an identity of our own and that it stands out musically.

Styles

Each region has its own musical style that is directly related to its culture.

In the area of styles, we will work on how these rhythms are traditionally created, such as analyzing, executing and merging them together; We will also see the changes they suffer from the adaptation to different instruments, or even the battery.

Painting and Drawing

Objectives

The importance of communication and its artistic-plastic expression, will help in the integral development of the person, enhancing personal capacity in the areas: Mathematical, Linguistic, Spatial, Musical, Corporal, Emotional and Sensory.

The creative process, developed in the workshops, will integrate:

  • Flexibility and diversity when it comes to understanding ideas.
  • Reflection and assessment of possible methods to use.
  • Autonomy to find a personalized answer.
  • Critical look of the environment and our proposals.

General Objectives

The contents, objectives and activities have been developed adapting them for adults, but this can also be made more flexible depending on the existing demand.

We can stand out:

  • Know and apply different painting procedures / techniques, (drawing, modeling, etc).
  • Recognize various materials and means of artistic expression and learn to use them properly.
  • Wake up and energize imagination and creativity.
  • Promote conceptual and emotional expression through plastic procedures.
  • Reflect and analyze the visual world around us, enhancing our sensibilities.
  • Develop visual and manual coordination.
  • Learn to develop routines and consolidate certain habits such as order, cleaning and conservation of work tools and materials, which will also lead us to know how to give value to things.
  • Discover the satisfaction of a shared leisure, respecting the work of the companions and collaborating in the group projects of the workshop.

Specific Objectives

With the development of each activity in the workshop, the aim will be to achieve several more concrete objectives related to the experiences that are carried out in each session:

  • Develop and enhance self-esteem and expression capacity through experimentation and plastic expression.
  • Awaken creativity, imagination, a taste for aesthetics and the spirit of self-improvement, through the imagination and development of ideas.
  • Increase visual perception and manual dexterity, through observation.
  • Enjoy manipulating and practicing with new and diverse materials, knowing the new possibilities that are presented to us and their expressive properties.
  • Interpret and value the personal creations and those of the companions with a critical and respectful attitude, in order to continue moving forward motivating us.
  • Be curious to discover and reinterpret elements of our near environment.
  • Raise awareness of habits and responsible uses of resources and recycling materials, exploring the artistic possibilities of objects and materials for everyday use.
